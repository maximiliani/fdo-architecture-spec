General Requirements

While the previous sections normatively specify the structure and required elements of FDO records, long-term operation of FDO-based infrastructures requires clarity on how FDO records may be updated over time. From an operational perspective, it is useful to distinguish between different classes of updates:

  1. Administrative updates, such as changes to service endpoints, responsible organisations, or access information, which do not affect the identity or interpretation of the referenced digital entity.
  2. Lifecycle and status updates, including embargo changes or the addition of a tombstone note, which increase transparency over the lifetime of an FDO.
  3. Provenance and relationship updates, where additional links to derived or related FDOs are added to improve traceability and reuse.
  4. Updates that would change the meaning or interpretation of the referenced bit-sequence(s)
The following requirements apply:
[R11-1]
It is RECOMMENDED that repositories operating FDO services document updates to FDO records through implementation-specific mechanisms (e.g. timestamps or internal change logs) to support auditability, trust, and long-term stewardship (documentation of points 1-3).
[R11-2]
It is RECOMMENDED that updates that would change the meaning or interpretation of the referenced bit-sequence(s) should normally result in the creation of a new FDO with a new PID rather than modification of the existing FDO record (point 4).

Special Requirements for profiles, attribute- and syntax definitions

FDO profiles, attribute- and syntax definitions play a central role in ensuring predictability and machine actionability of FDO records. From an operational and long-term stewardship perspective, it is important to consider the lifecycle of profiles, attribute- and syntax definitions beyond their initial creation and registration.

[R11-3]
Stability and immutability: Once a profile, attribute- or syntax definition is used productively to create FDO records, they effectively act as contracts between FDO creators, repositories, and consumers. To preserve trust, reproducibility, and long-term interpretability, any FDO profiles, attribute- and syntax definitions SHOULD be treated as stable and immutable artefacts.
[R11-4]
Profile evolution and versioning: Changes of attribute- or syntax definitions that affect the structure, cardinalities, or semantics of attributes SHOULD normally result in the creation of new profiles, attribute- or syntax definitions with new PIDs. Existing FDO records continue to reference the profile under which they were created. Minor editorial changes that do not affect validation or interpretation may be handled according to repository policies.
[R11-5]
Handling of legacy FDO records: Long-lived repositories are expected to manage FDO records created under earlier profile versions. Such records remain valid as long as the referenced profile remains resolvable. Optional mappings between profiles may be provided to support interoperability, but automatic migration of existing FDO records SHOULD NOT be assumed.
[R11-6]
Operational considerations: It is RECOMMENDED that repositories operating FDO services document profile versions, ensure persistent accessibility of profiles, and support validation of FDO records against the profiles they instantiate throughout their operational lifetime.
